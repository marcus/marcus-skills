## Tier 1: Elite / High-End Agencies

### Sandwich Video (Los Angeles)
**Comfort zone: $250,000 - $400,000 per project**

Founded by Adam Lisagor, Sandwich is widely regarded as the gold standard for tech product videos. They function as agency, production company, and post-production studio all in one.

**Notable Clients:** Slack, Square (Block), Airbnb, Warby Parker, Robinhood, Salesforce, Uber, Lyft, eBay, Starbucks, Rothy's, Notarize, Osmo

**What Makes Them Stand Out:**
- **Direct-to-camera approach**: Many spots feature an actor speaking directly to the viewer about the product -- friendly, approachable, confident casting is critical
- **Humor as a vehicle for clarity**: They wrap complex product explanations in genuinely funny, charming narratives
- **"Show the magic" philosophy**: Creative lead Sabrina Skau says great product videos showcase "the magic of the product... It doesn't have to be a big 'aha' moment -- it can be a series of moments"
- **Graphics serve clarity, not decoration**: "Graphics should always help to clarify for an audience how a product works" -- not add visual flair for its own sake
- **Minimalist style with passionate tone**: Videos are typically 1.5-2 minutes

**Production Process (roughly 8 weeks):**
1. Creative brainstorming and treatment development
2. Treatment collaboration with client -- making sure all critical product points are hit
3. Scripting -- distilling ideas from the treatment
4. Pre-production -- location scouting, casting, crew, storyboards, wardrobe, props
5. Production -- typically one day of shooting
6. Post-production (~1 month) -- editing, VFX, motion graphics, color correction, sound mix

**Unique Business Model:** Also runs Sandwich Fund (with Ludlow Ventures), investing in early-stage startups by funding launch video production in exchange for equity. This means they sometimes accept cash + equity + revenue sharing instead of full payment.

**Key Creative Decisions:**
- For Notarize: used split-screen to emphasize convenience benefits
- For Osmo: created four productions, with the "Masterpiece" entry avoiding voiceover entirely
- For Slack: visual representations of message communication flow
- Inclusive casting as a deliberate practice -- underrepresented groups in leading roles rather than stereotypical parts

## Cross-Agency Techniques That Stand Out

### Narrative & Script Techniques

1. **Problem-Solution-Proof structure**: Nearly universal. Open with a relatable pain point, introduce the product as the solution, show proof it works. The proportions matter -- Grumo Media emphasizes getting the ratio right.

2. **"Why" over "How" framing** (Thinkmojo/Slack approach): Don't show people how to click buttons. Show them why their life improves. Strategic narratives outperform instructional ones.

3. **"Show the magic" philosophy** (Sandwich): Identify the moments that make people go "oh that's cool" and build the video around those moments, not feature lists.

4. **Conversational tone**: Across all top agencies, scripts avoid corporate jargon. They read like one person explaining something to another.

5. **Role-specific targeting**: Create multiple versions of demo videos targeting different decision-makers within buying committees, each addressing their specific concerns.

### Visual & Production Techniques

6. **Hybrid animation (live-action + 2D)**: One of the biggest trends. Reduces production costs 30-40% while maintaining engagement. Epipheo pioneered this.

7. **Direct-to-camera presenter**: Sandwich's signature. A relatable person speaking to camera creates human connection that animation alone cannot.

8. **UI screen recordings with motion graphics overlay**: Combine actual interface captures with animated callouts, highlights, and transitions. Reduces cognitive load by up to 40% compared to raw screen recordings.

9. **Screencast animation**: Record the actual interface, then overlay pulsing/glowing highlights on clickable elements. Animation should feel natural, not distracting.

10. **Modular video design** (Venture Videos): Build videos so individual UI segments can be swapped when the product updates, without rebuilding the entire video. Critical for fast-moving SaaS.

11. **Multi-format delivery from single production**: One shoot/animation produces a 30s social cut, 90s homepage hero, 2-minute sales version, and vertical mobile format. This is now standard practice.

### Strategic Techniques

12. **Asset multiplication** (Vidico): Generate 5-10x more content from each production investment through systematic repurposing across the marketing funnel.

13. **Length matched to awareness level**:
    - New audiences: 60-90 seconds max
    - Interested prospects: 2-3 minutes with more detail
    - Evaluating buyers: 5+ minute deep dives

14. **Strong hook in first 5 seconds**: Address the pain point immediately. No logo animations or company introductions at the start.

15. **Social proof integration**: Weave in testimonials, logos, or data points rather than keeping them separate.

16. **ABM-personalized versions** (Thinkmojo/Zendesk): Create personalized video variants for specific target accounts in enterprise sales.

## Pricing Tiers & What You Get

### The Five Market Segments (2026)

| Tier | Price Range (60-90s) | What You Get | Best For |
|------|---------------------|--------------|----------|
| **AI & DIY Tools** | $50 - $500 | Template-based, generic, self-service | Internal comms, quick drafts |
| **Freelancers** | $1,000 - $3,500 | Variable quality, client manages project, basic animation | Budget-constrained startups |
| **Standard Studios** | $3,000 - $6,000 | Functional but lacks strategic depth, limited revisions | Early-stage with modest needs |
| **Professional Agencies** | $8,000 - $25,000 | Fully managed end-to-end (strategy, script, custom art, voiceover, sound), the "sweet spot" for B2B SaaS | Growth-stage SaaS, Series A+ |
| **Enterprise / Cinematic** | $25,000 - $100,000+ | Commercial-grade, film-quality production, celebrity talent possible | Major launches, brand campaigns |
| **Elite (Sandwich-tier)** | $250,000 - $400,000 | Full agency + production company, top-tier creative, live-action shoots | Category-defining brands |

### Key Cost Drivers

- **Strategic depth**: Lower tiers focus on execution; professional agencies add marketing consultation and positioning strategy
- **Turnaround time**: Standard is 6-10 weeks; expedited projects incur 20-50% premiums
- **Visual complexity**: Frame-by-frame animation and advanced 3D significantly increase labor hours
- **Revisions**: Most agencies include 2-3 rounds; additional rounds add 10-15% per round
- **Voiceover**: Professional talent adds $500-$3,000+ depending on usage rights and talent level
- **Music licensing**: Stock music $50-$500; custom composition $2,000-$10,000+

## Key Takeaways for Commissioning Work

### What separates great agencies from good ones:

1. **They start with strategy, not production**: The best agencies spend significant time understanding your product, audience, and positioning before writing a single word of script. Sandwich spends weeks on treatment and scripting before any cameras roll.

2. **They focus on "why" not "how"**: Instructional content is table stakes. Premium agencies create desire and understanding simultaneously, as Thinkmojo proved with Slack.

3. **They design for the full funnel**: One video isn't enough. Top agencies (Vidico, Venture Videos) build modular content systems that produce 5-10 assets from one production.

4. **They build for updatability**: Venture Videos' modular approach means you don't reshoot when your UI changes. This saves enormous money over time.

5. **They understand SaaS buyer psychology**: B2B buying committees have 6-10 people. The best agencies create role-specific variants that address each stakeholder's concerns.

### The professional agency sweet spot ($8,000 - $25,000):

For most growth-stage SaaS companies, the $8K-$25K range delivers the best ROI. You get end-to-end management (strategy, script, custom art, voiceover, sound design), professional quality that reflects well on your brand, and strategic messaging that drives conversions. Below this range, you're often managing the project yourself. Above it, you're paying for cinematic production values that may not move the needle more than a well-crafted animated explainer.

### Current trends to watch:

- **AI workflow visualization** is creating demand for specialized technical writers who can translate AI capabilities into visual narratives
- **Hybrid animation** (live-action + 2D) is reducing costs 30-40% while maintaining engagement
- **Multi-format delivery** (30s social, 90s homepage, 2min sales) is now standard, not a premium add-on
- **Subscription/retainer models** (pioneered by Superside) are challenging traditional per-project pricing
