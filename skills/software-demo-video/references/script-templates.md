# Script Templates and Frameworks

Ready-to-use script templates for every software video type. Pick the template, fill in brackets, read aloud, and time it.

---

## Word Count Reference

| Duration | Word Count | WPM Target |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| 30 seconds | ~75 words | 150 WPM |
| 60 seconds | 120-150 words | 130-150 WPM |
| 90 seconds | 180-225 words | 130-150 WPM |
| 2 minutes | 250-300 words | 130-150 WPM |
| 3 minutes | 375-450 words | 130-150 WPM |

For technical/jargon-heavy scripts, aim for 120-130 WPM to ensure comprehension.

## Framework Details

### Problem-Agitate-Solution (PAS)

The most widely used framework. Mirrors how people naturally think about problems.

```
[PROBLEM] "Managing remote teams through endless email chains creates chaos
and missed deadlines."

[AGITATE] "Every lost message means lost revenue. Every missed deadline means
frustrated clients. And your team? They're drowning."

[SOLUTION] "Meet [Product]. One workspace where your team communicates,
collaborates, and ships -- without the chaos."

[CTA] "Start your free trial today."
```

### Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

Shows transformation. Best for products with visually demonstrable outcomes.

```
BEFORE: "Here's what your world looks like today." (Show the pain)
AFTER:  "Here's what it could look like." (Show the outcome)
BRIDGE: "Here's how [Product] gets you there." (Show the product)
```

### Tell-Show-Tell

Three-phase method that increases retention by up to 65%.

| Phase | Duration | What Happens |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Tell | 30-60s | Outline what the audience will see and why it matters |
| Show | 3-5 min per segment | Live product demonstration with real-world scenarios |
| Tell | 30-60s | Reinforce value, link features to business impact |

For complex products, break into multiple Tell-Show-Tell modules rather than one long demo.

### Hero's Journey (Adapted)

Position the customer as the hero, your product as the guide.

```
ACT I -- The Ordinary World (Setup)
  "Sarah manages a 50-person sales team. Every quarter, forecasting
  takes two weeks of spreadsheet wrangling."

ACT II -- The Journey (Discovery)
  "Then Sarah found [Product]. In her first week, she..."
  [This is where the demo lives. Show the product solving real problems.]

ACT III -- The Return (Resolution)
  "Now Sarah's team forecasts in hours, not weeks. They closed
  23% more deals last quarter."
```

Your company is NOT the hero. The customer is.

### Job-to-be-Done (JTBD)

Centers on the outcome, not the features.

```
SITUATION:  "When [situation]..."
MOTIVATION: "I want to [job to be done]..."
OUTCOME:    "So that [desired outcome]..."
PRODUCT:    "[Product] does exactly this by [mechanism]."
```

Example:
```
"When your engineering team ships a new release, you want to know
instantly if anything breaks -- so your customers never notice a problem.
[Product] monitors every deployment in real time and alerts you in
Slack before users are impacted."
```

### Hook-Story-Offer

Concise three-part framework for short-form video.

```
HOOK:  Capture attention with bold question or claim (2-5 sec)
STORY: Engage with a mini-narrative showing the journey (20-40 sec)
OFFER: Close with clear call-to-action (5-10 sec)
```

### AIDA

| Stage | Purpose | Video Application |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| Attention | Grab focus | Shocking stat or bold visual |
| Interest | Build relevance | Show the problem is real |
| Desire | Create want | Demonstrate the product solving it |
| Action | Drive conversion | Clear CTA with next step |

### Pixar Story Spine

```
"Once upon a time... [status quo]"
"Every day... [routine/problem]"
"One day... [discovery of product]"
"Because of that... [first improvement]"
"Because of that... [compounding benefit]"
"Until finally... [transformed outcome]"
```

### Golden Circle (Sinek)

```
WHY:  "We believe teams should spend time on work that matters,
       not manual data entry."
HOW:  "We built an AI that automates repetitive workflows so your
       team can focus on strategy."
WHAT: "Our platform saves teams an average of 200 hours per month."
```

## Video Type Templates

### 60-Second Explainer (~150 words)

```
[HOOK -- 5 seconds, ~15 words]
"What if you could [achieve desired outcome] without [pain point]?"

[PROBLEM -- 15 seconds, ~35 words]
"Right now, [target audience] spend [time/money] on [painful activity].
It's [frustrating/slow/expensive], and it's holding your team back
from [what they actually want to do]."

[AGITATE -- 10 seconds, ~25 words]
"And the longer you wait, the more [negative consequence].
Your competitors are already [moving ahead / solving this]."

[SOLUTION -- 20 seconds, ~45 words]
"[Product] is [brief description in plain language].
Simply [key action 1], and [Product] automatically [key action 2].
In [timeframe], you'll [specific outcome]."

[SOCIAL PROOF -- 5 seconds, ~15 words]
"[Number] teams already use [Product] to [key benefit]."

[CTA -- 5 seconds, ~15 words]
"Start your free trial at [URL]. No credit card required."
```

### 90-Second SaaS Explainer (~220 words)

```
[HOOK -- 5 seconds]
Bold question or surprising statistic.

[PROBLEM -- 20 seconds]
Describe the status quo pain. Use "you" to make it personal.
Show what the viewer's day looks like with this problem.

[AGITATE -- 15 seconds]
Escalate the consequences. What does this cost in time, money,
or frustration? What happens if nothing changes?

[SOLUTION INTRO -- 10 seconds]
"That's why we built [Product]. [One-sentence description]."

[KEY FEATURES -- 25 seconds]
Show 2-3 key features in action. For each:
  - Name the feature (2 sec)
  - Show it working (5 sec)
  - State the benefit (3 sec)

[SOCIAL PROOF -- 10 seconds]
Customer quote, metric, or logo bar.
"[Company] reduced [problem metric] by [percentage] in [timeframe]."

[CTA -- 5 seconds]
"Try [Product] free for 14 days. Set up in 2 minutes."
```

### Product Tour (2-3 minutes, ~300-400 words)

```
[COLD OPEN -- 10 seconds]
Start with the outcome. Show the end result first.
"This is what your dashboard looks like after [Product]
is set up. Let me show you how to get here."

[OVERVIEW -- 30 seconds]
High-level product description. What is it? Who is it for?
Show the main interface or home screen.

[WORKFLOW 1 -- 45 seconds]
TELL: "First, let's look at [workflow]. This is where you [task]."
SHOW: Demonstrate the workflow with realistic data.
TELL: "So now you've [outcome] in [timeframe] instead of [old way]."

[WORKFLOW 2 -- 45 seconds]
Same Tell-Show-Tell pattern for second key workflow.

[WORKFLOW 3 -- 45 seconds]
Same pattern for third key workflow.

[RESULTS -- 15 seconds]
"Teams using [Product] typically see [metric 1], [metric 2],
and [metric 3]."

[CTA -- 10 seconds]
"Ready to try it? Start your free trial at [URL],
or book a personalized demo with our team."
```

### Feature Highlight (45-60 seconds, ~100-130 words)

```
[HOOK -- 5 seconds]
"Here's how [Product] helps you [job to be done]."

[CONTEXT -- 10 seconds]
"When you're [situation where this feature matters], you need
[capability]. That's exactly what [Feature Name] does."

[DEMO -- 25 seconds]
Show the feature in action. Use realistic data.
Narrate the purpose of each step, not every click.
3-5 actions maximum.

[BENEFIT -- 10 seconds]
"Instead of [old way], you now [new outcome].
That's [time saved / improvement metric]."

[CTA -- 5 seconds]
"Try it now in your [Product] account, or start a free trial."
```

### Customer Story (60-90 seconds, ~150-200 words)

```
[INTRO -- 10 seconds]
"I'm [Name], [Role] at [Company]. We [brief company description]."

[CHALLENGE -- 20 seconds]
"Before [Product], we were struggling with [specific problem].
We tried [alternatives], but [why they fell short].
It was costing us [specific impact: time/money/frustration]."

[DISCOVERY -- 15 seconds]
"When we found [Product], what stood out was [key differentiator].
We could see right away that [initial impression]."

[RESULTS -- 25 seconds]
"Since implementing [Product], we've [measurable result 1].
Our team [measurable result 2]. And we've [measurable result 3].
The biggest surprise was [unexpected benefit]."

[RECOMMENDATION -- 10 seconds]
"If you're dealing with [problem], I'd absolutely recommend
[Product]. It [one-sentence summary of value]."
```

Interview questions to prompt authentic responses:
- "What was your biggest challenge before using [Product]?"
- "What made you choose [Product] over alternatives?"
- "How much time or money did you save?"
- "What surprised you most?"
- "What would you tell someone considering [Product]?"

### Comparison Video (2-3 minutes, ~300-400 words)

```
[HOOK -- 10 seconds]
"Trying to decide between [Product] and [Competitor]?
Here's an honest breakdown to help you choose."

[FRAMING -- 20 seconds]
"Both are [category] tools, but they take different approaches.
Let's compare them on [3-4 criteria that matter most]."

[CRITERION 1 -- 30 seconds]
"For [criterion]: [Competitor] does [X]. [Product] does [Y].
If you need [specific need], [Product] is stronger here."

[CRITERION 2 -- 30 seconds]
Same structure.

[CRITERION 3 -- 30 seconds]
Same structure.

[HONEST VERDICT -- 20 seconds]
"[Competitor] is better if you need [specific thing].
[Product] is better if you need [specific thing].
For most [target audience], [Product] wins because [reason]."

[CTA -- 10 seconds]
"Try both and decide for yourself. Start your free trial at [URL]."
```

Acknowledge competitor strengths. Honest analysis builds more credibility than unfairly disparaging alternatives.

### Launch/Announcement (30-90 seconds)

```
[HOOK -- 5 seconds]
Tease what's coming.

[CONTEXT -- 10 seconds]
Why this matters / what problem it addresses.

[REVEAL -- 30 seconds]
Show the new feature/product in action.

[IMPACT -- 15 seconds]
What this means for users.

[CTA -- 10 seconds]
Try it, learn more, join waitlist.
```

## Content Strategy

### What to Show vs. Skip

| Show | Skip |
|------|------|
| Features solving the #1 pain point | Configuration/settings screens |
| Visually impressive workflows | Login/signup flows (unless that IS the feature) |
| "Aha moment" differentiators | Every menu option |
| Clear before/after impact | Edge cases and advanced customization |
| Integrations viewers already use | Backend/admin features (unless targeting admins) |

### Handling Complex Workflows

1. Break into segments -- multiple Tell-Show-Tell modules
2. Progressive disclosure -- overview first, then details
3. Visual anchors -- consistent highlights, callouts, zoom effects
4. Narrate the "why" over the "how" -- skip click-by-click
5. Time-compress repetitive steps -- speed up or skip routine actions
6. Limit to 3-5 key steps per video

### Emotional vs. Rational (B2B)

95% of buying behavior is influenced by subconscious emotional processing. Lead with emotion, justify with data.

| | B2B | B2C |
|--|-----|-----|
| Script balance | 60% emotional / 40% rational | 80% emotional / 20% rational |
| Effective framing | "Sleep better knowing your data is protected" | "Look amazing in every photo" |

### Social Proof Integration

| Type | Placement |
|------|-----------|
| Customer logos | After product introduction |
| Metrics ("50,000+ teams") | Opening hook or mid-video |
| Short testimonial clips (10-15s) | After feature demonstration |
| Case study snippets | During solution section |
| Integration logos | During solution section |

## CTA Best Practices

### Placement

| Position | Performance |
|----------|------------|
| Pre-roll (beginning) | Lowest conversion |
| **Mid-roll (middle)** | **Highest conversion** |
| Post-roll (end) | Strong for committed viewers |

Video CTAs average ~16% conversion rate (Wistia). Video CTAs increase conversion by up to 80%.

### Language

| Category | Phrases |
|----------|---------|
| Free trial | "Start your free trial" / "Try it free" / "Get started free" |
| Demo | "Book a demo" / "See it in action" |
| Sign up | "Get started" / "Get instant access" |

**Rules:**
1. Match CTA to video type (explainer = "Learn more"; demo = "Start free trial")
2. Say it in voiceover AND show as text/button
3. Use first-person for buttons ("Start my free trial")
4. Add friction-reducers ("No credit card required" / "Set up in 2 minutes")

### Creating Urgency Without Being Pushy

```
PUSHY:  "Sign up NOW before this offer expires!"
BETTER: "Teams that started this quarter are already seeing 30%
         faster deploys. Where will you be next quarter?"
```

Implied urgency outperforms explicit urgency. Show what peers are achieving instead of demanding action.
