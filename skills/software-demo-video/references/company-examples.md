# Company Examples

What the best software companies do in their product videos, broken down into specific techniques you can learn from and apply.

---

## Apple -- The Gold Standard

### What They Do

**Beat-synced editing.** Every cut, text reveal, and camera movement lands on a musical beat. Creates a physical, almost hypnotic rhythm. Opening tease: 2-3s cuts. Feature deep-dives: 5-8s holds. Finale: rapid cutting with music crescendo.

**Macro close-ups with shallow DOF.** Products filmed at extreme close range showing material textures. Camera starts macro and pulls back to reveal -- creating discovery.

**Controlled camera movement.** All motion is mechanized (robotic arms, motion-controlled rigs). Slow orbital rotations, vertical lifts, tracking shots, push-ins timed to music hits. Never handheld.

**Black/white void backgrounds.** Product is the only thing in frame. The screen becomes the primary color source.

**Custom foley sound design.** Every interaction has a designed sound: MagSafe click, keyboard snap, display whoosh. Exaggerated for impact.

**No raw screen recordings.** Apple re-creates UI in motion graphics, films devices at angles with UI visible, animates between states with custom transitions, or shows UI "floating" above the device in 3D.

**Text animation.** Kinetic typography in San Francisco font. Oversized numerals for specs. Text appears, lands, and vanishes in 2-3 seconds.

### Lesson
Music sync creates emotion that bypasses rational evaluation. Treat every product video like a short film.

## Stripe -- Code as Art

### What They Do

**Code as visual composition.** Custom-syntax-highlighted blocks with curated palettes. Code is always real and functional.

**Animation hierarchy.** CSS Transitions > CSS Animations > Web Animations API > requestAnimationFrame. Custom cubic-bezier easing. Only animate transform/opacity. Durations under 500ms.

**3D visual metaphors.** Rotating cubes as "building blocks" for API concepts. Dynamic lighting from virtual light source makes abstract infrastructure tangible.

**CSS-rendered devices.** Laptop/phone mockups drawn in CSS -- under 1KB, resolution-independent, with interactive content inside.

**Developer advocates write scripts.** Technical authenticity because the explainer is the expert.

### Lesson
Developer tools don't need dumbing down to be visually compelling. Treat code and APIs as aesthetic objects.

## Linear -- Product IS the Video

### What They Do

**Dark-themed foundation.** #08090a background. Navigation sidebar slightly dimmer than content. Natural hierarchy without borders.

**Micro-animations.** CSS keyframes at 2800-3200ms intervals. "Living" interface without being distracting.

**The interface does the talking.** Product is so visually refined that recordings feel cinematic. Smooth animations, crisp typography, and dark theme give raw footage a premium feel.

**Outcome-oriented language.** "Capture ideas," "delegate issues," "track progress" -- never technical implementation.

**Minimal post-production.** The product's own transitions provide visual interest.

### Lesson
If your product is beautiful enough, the simplest video treatment is the most effective. Only works when the underlying design is exceptional.

## Figma -- Making Collaboration Visible

### What They Do

**Animated multi-cursors.** Multiple labeled cursors moving simultaneously. Makes the abstract concept of "collaboration" physically visible.

**UI click sounds.** "Clicky sound effects that add pop." Every drag, drop, resize gets a subtle audio cue. Transforms silent recordings into engaging audiovisual experiences.

**Config rapid-fire format.** Quick feature showcases with minimal voiceover. Product interface does the talking. Fast sequencing with upbeat music.

**Vertical video (3:4).** Mobile-first carousel-based demos on their website.

### Lesson
Make invisible capabilities physically visible. Sound design transforms passive viewing into something almost tactile.

## Arc Browser -- Authenticity Over Everything

### What They Do

**CEO in bedroom with webcam.** Lighting "a bit off," composition "not the best." Entirely deliberate -- makes viewers "forget they are watching content made by a company."

**YouTuber energy.** Direct, energetic, personal. Creates intimacy common with YouTubers but unheard of in corporate communication.

**3-4 videos per month.** Content creator cadence, not marketing department cadence.

**Team transparency.** Actual engineers discuss challenges and creative processes.

**Community as co-creators.** User feedback drives priorities; videos make this explicit.

### Lesson
Authenticity beats production value. A well-told story in 720p beats a boring story in 4K. Costs dramatically less while generating higher engagement.

## Descript -- Comedy as Demo

### What They Do

**Comedy sketch format.** Personal opening, every feature demoed within a narrative context, fourth-wall breaks, intentional chaos.

**"This video isn't afraid to be chaotic."** Yet every feature is clearly visible despite comedic framing.

**Full funnel in one video.** Targets awareness, consideration, and conversion simultaneously.

**High production value.** Professional actors, scripted comedy, precise timing despite "chaotic" feel. ~$75K+ production cost.

### Lesson
Match creative ambition to audience. Descript's audience is creators who appreciate video production. Comedy sketches signal "we understand your world."

## Slack -- Problem-First

### What They Do

**Problem-solution structure.** Opens acknowledging communication chaos. Product appears as organizer. Humor establishes immediately: "We ask ourselves that every day."

**Mockumentary approach.** Case study videos in The Office style. Visual metaphors: morphing inboxes, document transformations.

**Custom-built screenshots.** Idealized but realistic product states with screencast animation. Avoids real data being messy or distracting.

**Hints at brand values without stating them.** Flexibility, transparency, inclusion -- shown, not told.

### Lesson
Start with the viewer's pain. By the time the product appears, the viewer already wants the solution.

## Notion -- Scenarios Over Features

### What They Do

**Scenario-based narratives.** "Go from brainstorm to roadmap," "Turn meetings into social posts." Use cases as relatable stories.

**Named animation system.** "noseyAgents," "noseyGlasses" -- playful mascot characters guide through features.

**Warm, approachable tone.** Closer to a knowledgeable friend than a salesperson.

**Social proof woven into narrative.** Not separate testimonial videos -- supporting evidence within the product story.

### Lesson
Never show a blank canvas. Start with a specific person trying to do a specific thing. The product appears as the natural solution.

## Other Notable Approaches

### Superhuman -- Restraint as Technique
Premium $30/mo email client. Custom fonts (Super Sans, Super Serif, Super Mono). Favors static imagery over embedded video. Scarcity of content creates exclusivity. Each piece is so refined it reinforces premium positioning.

### Loom -- Meta-Product Demo
Product IS the demo tool. Every demo simultaneously demonstrates product quality. Webcam + screen recording format has become a convention other companies imitate.

### Raycast -- Developer Audience Mastery
Keyboard visualization (ASCII keyboard displays). Features well-known developers' setups (Wes Bos). Glass-morphism dark aesthetic. In-group signaling that makes target audience feel "this was built for me."

### Vercel -- Metrics Over UI
Leads with performance numbers: "Build times went from 7m to 40s." Interactive globe visualization makes edge computing visible. Developers don't want to watch clicks; they want to see results.

### Salesforce -- Hybrid Mini-Movie
Opens with business challenge, introduces solution rapidly. Functions as product demo AND explainer simultaneously. Quirky personality ("Power Poofs") makes enterprise memorable. ~$55K+.

### Island -- Live-Action B2B
Philosophical opening ("What is a browser?"). Real actors, not motion graphics. Treats B2B SaaS as short film. "Creativity in B2B SaaS isn't a risk; it's the thing that makes you memorable." ~$100K+.

### Duolingo -- Mascot Personality
Fast-paced 2D animation. Mascot delivers "unhinged" narration. Heart/streak visuals create in-jokes with existing users while accessible to new ones.

## Cross-Company Patterns

### How Top Companies Handle "Boring" Features

| Strategy | Examples | Technique |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Motion graphics overlay | Most SaaS | Animate data flows, transitions, abstractions |
| Speed-up, don't cut | Canva, Process Street | Film naturally, accelerate tedious parts 2-4x |
| Scenario storytelling | Notion, Monday, Slack | Embed features in relatable workplace stories |
| Skip entirely | Linear, Superhuman | Only show features that look good; imply completeness |

## Viral Patterns

**"It Just Works" moment.** A single moment where complex technology produces an effortless result. Adobe Firefly's instant image generation, Figma's multiplayer, Copilot's code completion.

**Personality over polish.** Arc's vlogs, Descript's comedy, Duolingo's social content outperform polished competitors. People share content that feels human.

**Speed demonstrations.** Vercel's build times, Raycast's keyboard shortcuts, Superhuman's email processing. Speed is viscerally satisfying and inherently shareable.

**Before/after transformations.** Slack's "email hell to organized channels," Notion's "scattered to unified." Viewers share as aspirational content.

**Developer celebrity endorsements.** Well-known developers sharing workflows spreads through professional networks.

**The ChatGPT template.** Simple screen recording, no narration, no music -- just the product doing something surprising. Zero production cost, infinite shareability.

## Agency Techniques Worth Knowing

### Sandwich Video ($250K-400K)
"Show the magic" philosophy. Direct-to-camera presenter. Humor as vehicle for clarity. Graphics serve clarity, not decoration. ~8 week process.

### Thinkmojo (Enterprise)
"Why" over "How." Deep client integration (joined Slack as guest). ABM-personalized variants for enterprise accounts.

### Vidico (Global)
"Asset-multiplying" -- 5-10x content from each production. 1.4B+ total views. 15-42% conversion improvements.

### Venture Videos (SaaS)
Modular design so UI updates don't require full rebuilds. Performance over vanity metrics.

### Key Pricing
- Professional agency sweet spot: $8K-$25K (strategy + script + art + VO + sound)
- Hybrid animation (live + 2D) reduces cost 30-40% while maintaining engagement
- Multi-format delivery (30s social, 90s homepage, 2min sales) is now standard, not premium
