About

Elena Vasquez is a documentary and editorial photographer based between Mexico City and New York. Her work examines the intersection of private ritual and public space, drawing on the traditions of street photography while incorporating the deliberate pace and material attention of large-format practice.

Over the past two decades, her projects have taken her from the copper canyons of northern Mexico to the post-industrial landscapes of the American Midwest. She works primarily in black and white, favoring silver gelatin and platinum palladium processes for their tonal depth and archival permanence.

Her photographs are held in the permanent collections of the Museum of Modern Art, the Tate Modern, and the Centro de la Imagen, Mexico City.
