Parklet Design System

A design system for discovering neighborhoods — warm, curious, and built with care.

Brand

Parklet is a curated guide to a city's hidden gems — the coffee shops that roast their own beans, the bookstores where the owner still writes shelf notes, the parks with the best afternoon light, the street art that changes every season.

We built Parklet for urban explorers who are discerning about quality but approach discovery with childlike curiosity. People who trust recommendations from someone who has actually been there, not from an algorithm optimizing for engagement.

Every neighborhood has a secret. Parklet helps you find it.

Curious, not nosy

We explore with genuine interest and share what we find. We never pry, never intrude, never turn a quiet spot into a spectacle. Curiosity guided by respect.

Warm, not cute

Approachable and genuine — never saccharine, never resorting to twee illustrations or forced friendliness. Warmth that feels like a real conversation.

Opinionated, not exclusive

We have taste and stand by our picks. But our door is open to everyone. Opinionated curation that invites you in rather than shutting you out.

Crafted, not polished

Intentional and considered at every level, but never sterile or over-produced. The character of handmade work — a slight irregularity that proves a human was here.

Principles

Design Principles

01

Neighborhood first

The neighborhood is the protagonist. Every design decision serves the content — the places, the stories, the texture of a block. The system stays out of the way so the neighborhood can speak.

02

Delight in the details

A spring-eased hover. A hand-drawn pin-drop. A line of type that settles into its space with care. Delight lives in the small interactions — the moments that reward attention without demanding it.

03

Clear hierarchy, easy wandering

Structure should guide without constraining. Users always know where they are and how to get somewhere else — but they also feel free to wander, to follow a tangent, to get happily lost.

04

Let content breathe

Generous spacing is not wasted space. It is the typographic equivalent of a quiet street — room to notice things. Give content the vertical rhythm it needs to be read, not just scanned.

05

Accessible by default

Every interaction, every color pairing, every motion decision starts with accessibility. Not as an afterthought, not as a compliance checkbox — as the foundation. If it is not accessible, it is not done.

Shape

Shape Language

Parklet's radius hierarchy is warmer and rounder than typical agency work. The playful brand earns softer corners — buttons at 12px, cards at 16px, features at 24px.

none 0
sm 6px
md 12px
lg 16px
xl 24px
pill 9999px
Nested radius rule: When a rounded element sits inside another rounded element, the inner radius should be outer radius - padding. A card with radius-lg (16px) and 24px padding would have inner elements at radius-none. A card with 16px radius and 8px padding would have inner elements at 8px radius.
Motion

Motion

Parklet uses spring easing more liberally than typical systems. The playful overshoot is part of the brand personality — interactions should feel buoyant, not mechanical.

Easing Curves

cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.1, 0.25, 1.0)

Symmetric transitions. Modals, drawers, elements entering and leaving with equal weight.

Parklet's durations run slightly longer than tool-focused systems like Linear or Raycast. The brand is about wandering, not efficiency — animations need room to breathe. Spring easing at 350ms feels buoyant. At 150ms it would feel twitchy.

Motif

Signature Motif

An organic pin-drop / leaf hybrid — the shape of dropping a pin on a map, but with the softness of something that grew there naturally.

The pin-drop shape is Parklet's visual signature. It is created with border-radius: 50% 50% 50% 0 and a 45-degree rotation, producing a form that sits between a map marker and a leaf. The inner cutout reinforces the pin metaphor while creating visual lightness.

Usage Contexts

  • Location markers on maps and neighborhood guides
  • Decorative accents in hero sections and feature callouts
  • Loading states — the pin-drop animates with the spring curve
  • List markers for curated recommendations
  • Favicon and app icon foundation
  • Background pattern at low opacity for empty states
